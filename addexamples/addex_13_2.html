Additional Examples 13.2

1 Use Newton's Method to find the minimum of \(f(x,y) = x^2+y^2+\sin (x+3y).\)


The gradient of \(f \) is \(\nabla f (x,y) = \left[\begin{array}{c} 2x+\cos (x+3y)\\2y+3\cos (x+3y) \end{array}\right] \) and the Hessian is \( H_f(x,y) = \left[\begin{array}{cc} 2-\sin (x+3y)&-3\sin (x+3y)\\-3\sin (x+3y)&2-9\sin (x+3y) \end{array}\right]. \)

The Newton Method iteration is \(w_{k+1} = w_k+v\) where \(H_f(w_k)v = -\nabla f (w).\) Using the initial guess \(w_0 = [0,0]\), the iteration converges to \((x,y)= (-0.130644, -0.391932)\) after 11 steps. The minimum value of \(f\) is \(-0.794582.\)

2 Apply Conjugate Gradient Search to find the minimum of \(f(x,y,z) = x^2+x+y^2+2y+z^2+3z-e^{-x^2-y^2-z^2}.\)

The gradient is \(\nabla f = \left[\begin{array}{c} 2x+1+2xe^{-x^2-y^2-z^2}\\2y+2+2ye^{-x^2-y^2-z^2}\\2z+3+2ze^{-x^2-y^2-z^2} \end{array}\right].\) Apply Conjugate Gradient with Successive Parabolic Interpolation as the one-dimensional optimizer. The solution after 4 steps is \((x,y,z) = (-0.4812,-0.9624,-1.4436)\) at which \(f\) has the minimum value \(-3.5341.\)
