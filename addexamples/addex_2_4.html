Additional Examples 2.4

1 Find the \(PA=LU\) factorization of the matrix \( A=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&2\\3&4 \end{array}\right]. \)

Partial pivoting requires exchanging the rows using \( P=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 0&1\\1&0 \end{array}\right]. \) Subtracting \( 1/3\) times row one from row two yields \( U=\left[\begin{array}{cc} 3&4\\0&2/3 \end{array}\right], \) yielding the \( PA=LU\) factorization \[ \left[\begin{array}{rr} 0&1\\1&0 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&2\\3&4 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{cc} 1&0\\1/3&1 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{cc} 3&4\\0&2/3 \end{array}\right] \]
2 Solve \begin{align*} \left[\begin{array}{rrr} -1&1&-2\\ 2&3&1\\ 4&8&-4\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rrr} x_1\\x_2\\x_3 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rrr} -5\\2\\-4 \end{array}\right] \end{align*} using the PA=LU factorization with partial pivoting.


The steps involved in the PA=LU factorization are: \begin{align*} \left[\begin{array}{rrr} -1&1&-2\\ 2&3&1\\ 4&8&-4\\ \end{array}\right] \longrightarrow \stackrel{ \textstyle{P= \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 0&0&1\\ 0&1&0\\ 1&0&0\\ \end{array}\right] }}{\mbox{ exchange rows 1 and 3}} \longrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 4&8&-4\\ 2&3&1\\ -1&1&-2\\ \end{array}\right] \end{align*} \begin{align*}& \longrightarrow \stackrel{\textstyle{\mbox{subtract $\frac{1}{2}$ x row 1}}}{\mbox{from row 2}} \longrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 4&8&-4\\ {\frac{1}{2}}&-1&3\\ -1&1&-2\\ \end{array}\right] \end{align*} \begin{align*} & \longrightarrow \stackrel{\textstyle{\mbox{subtract \(-\frac{1}{4}\) x row 1}}}{\mbox{from row 3}} \longrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 4&8&-4\\ {\frac{1}{2}}&-1&3\\ {-\frac{1}{4}}&3&-3\\ \end{array}\right] \end{align*} \begin{align*} %\left[\begin{array}{rrr} %4&8&-4\\ %\frac{1}{2}&-1&3\\ %-\frac{1}{4}&3&-3\\ %\end{array}\right] \longrightarrow \stackrel{ \textstyle{ P= \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 0&0&1\\ 1&0&0\\ 0&1&0\\ \end{array}\right] }}{\mbox{ exchange rows 2 and 3}} \longrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 4&8&-4\\ -\frac{1}{4}&3&-3\\ \frac{1}{2}&-1&3\\ \end{array}\right] \end{align*} \begin{align*} & \longrightarrow \stackrel{\textstyle{\mbox{subtract $-\frac{1}{3}$ x row 2}}}{\mbox{from row 3}} \longrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 4&8&-4\\ -\frac{1}{4}&3&-3\\ \frac{1}{2}&-\frac{1}{3}&2\\ \end{array}\right] \end{align*} The PA=LU factorization is: \[\left[\begin{array}{rrr} 0&0&1\\ 1&0&0\\ 0&1&0\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rrr} -1&1&-2\\ 2&3&1\\ 4&8&-4 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1&0&0\\ -\frac{1}{4}&1&0\\ \frac{1}{2}&-\frac{1}{3}&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 4&8&-4\\ 0&3&-3\\ 0&0&2\\ \end{array}\right]\] Next we apply the two backsubstitution steps. First, solve \(Lc=Pb\): \[ \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1&0&0\\ -\frac{1}{4}&1&0\\ \frac{1}{2}&-\frac{1}{3}&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} c_1\\ c_2\\ c_3\\ \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 0&0&1\\ 1&0&0\\ 0&1&0\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} -5\\ 2\\ -4\\ \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r} -4\\ -5\\ 2\\ \end{array}\right] \] Starting at the top, \begin{align*} c_1&= -4\\ -\frac{1}{4}(-4)+c_2&= -5 \ \ \ \Rightarrow \ \ \ c_2 = -6\\ \frac{1}{2}(-4)-\frac{1}{3}(-6)+c_3&= 2 \ \ \ \Rightarrow \ \ \ c_3 = 2 \end{align*} Secondly, solve \(Ux=c\): \[ \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 4&8&-4\\ 0&3&-3\\ 0&0&2\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} x_1\\ x_2\\ x_3\\ \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r} -4\\ -6\\ 2\\ \end{array}\right] \] Starting at the bottom, \begin{align*} 2x_3&= 2 \quad \Rightarrow\quad x_3= 1 \\ 3x_2-3(1)&= -6 \quad\Rightarrow\quad x_2 = -1\\ 4x_1+8(-1)-4(1)&= -4 \quad\Rightarrow\quad x_1 = 2 \end{align*} Therefore the solution is \(x=[2,-1,1]\).
