2 Project the vectors \[ \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\-3\\-1\\4\\1 \end{array}\right], \ \ \ \ \left[\begin{array}{r} 8\\-14\\-1\\11\\1 \end{array}\right], \ \ \ \ \left[\begin{array}{r} 2\\-1\\1\\-2\\3 \end{array}\right], \ \ \ \ \left[\begin{array}{r} -1\\-2\\-6\\13\\5 \end{array}\right] \] onto the best least squares plane in \({\mathbb R}^5.\)

Form the matrix \[ A=\left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 1& 8& 2& -1\\ -3& -14& -1& -2\\ -1& -1& 1& -6\\ 4 & 11& -2& 13\\ 1& 1& 3& 5 \end{array}\right] \] and find its SVD \(A=USV^T.\) The best plane is spanned by \(u_1\) and \(u_2,\) the first two columns of \(U.\) The projection of the four vectors onto this plane is given by the columns of \(s_{11}u_1v_1^T+s_{22}u_2v_2^T, \) or equivalently by setting \(s_{33}=s_{44}=0\) in \(S\) and recomputing \(USV^T.\) The four projected vectors are the columns of the resulting matrix \[ A' = \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 1.2585& 8.0545& 1.0113& -1.1969\\ -2.8365& -14.0184& -1.1073& -2.0426\\ -1.1137& -0.9645& 0.8518& -6.0057\\ 4.0163& 10.8920& -0.9707& 13.1603\\ 0.9428& 1.3739& -0.5631& 4.4452 \end{array}\right] \]
