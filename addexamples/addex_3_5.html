Additional Examples 3.5

1 Assume that a one-piece Bezier spline has endpoints \( (0,2)\) and \( (0, -1)\), and horizontal tangents at both endpoints. Find all possible points \( (x(1/3),y(1/3)) \) on such a curve.
Horizontal tangents means that the control points are \((A,2), (A,-1)\) for some \(A.\) Then the coefficients of the Bezier cubic polynomials can be directly calculated: \(b_x = 3A, c_x = -3A, d_x = 0, b_y = 0, c_y = -9,\) and \(d_y = 6.\) The Bezier curve is \begin{eqnarray*} x(t) &=& 3At-3At^2\\ y(t) &=& 2-9t^2+6t^3 \end{eqnarray*} and substituting \(t=1/3,\) \begin{eqnarray*} x(1/3) &=& \frac{2}{3}A\\ y(1/3) &=& \frac{11}{9} \end{eqnarray*} Therefore the set of all possible points \((x(1/3), y(1/3))\) is the line \(y=11/9.\)
2 Assume that a one-piece Bezier spline has endpoints \( (0,-1)\) and \( (0, 1)\), and control points \( (A,-1)\) and \( (A, 1)\) for some real number \(A\). Find the maximum \( x\)-coordinate on the curve in terms of \(A\).
Note that if \(A\le 0,\) the Bezier curve lies in the left half-plane, and the maximum \(x\)-coordinate is \(0\) occurring at the endpoints.

If \(A\ge 0,\) the curve lies in the right half-plane. The coefficients of the Bezier cubic polynomials can be directly calculated: \(b_x = 3A, c_x = -3A, d_x = 0, b_y = 0, c_y = 6,\) and \(d_y = -4.\) The Bezier curve is \begin{eqnarray*} x(t) &=& 3At-3At^2 = 3At(1-t)\\ y(t) &=& -1+6t^2-4t^3. \end{eqnarray*} The maximum \(x\)-coordinate occurs at \(t=1/2,\) with value \(3A\frac{1}{2}(1-\frac{1}{2}) = \frac{3}{4}A.\)
