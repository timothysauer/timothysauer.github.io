Additional Examples 2.7

1 Use Multivariate Newton's Method to find the intersection points in \(R^2\) of the circle of radius 2 centered at the origin, and the circle of radius 1 centered at \( (1,1)\).

The Matlab code 
n=10;
x=[1;0];
f=@(x) [x(1)^2+x(2)^2-4;(x(1)-1)^2+(x(2)-1)^2-1];
df=@(x) 2*[x(1) x(2);x(1)-1 x(2)-1];
for i=1:n
  v=df(x)\f(x);
  x=x-v
end
uses the initial guess \((1,0)\) to locate the root \((1.9114,0.5886)\). Using the initial guess \((0,1),\) the second root \((0.5886,1.9114)\) is located.

2 Use the Broyden II method to find the two intersection points in \(R^3\) of three spheres: the sphere of radius 2 centered at the origin, and the two spheres of radius 1 centered at \( (1,1,1)\) and \( (1,1,0) \), respectively.

Using initial guess \((0,1,1)\), the broyden2.py code finds the intersection point \((1.8090,0.6910, 0.5000)\) from the commands 
f=lambda x: [x[0]**2+x[1]**2+x[2]**2-4.,(x[0]-1)**2+(x[1]-1)**2+(x[2]-1)**2-1,(x[0]-1)**2+(x[1]-1)**2+x[2]**2-1];
x=broyden2(f,np.array([0,1,1]),15)
Using initial guess \((1,0,1)\), the other intersection point \((0.6910,1.8090, 0.5000)\) is located.
