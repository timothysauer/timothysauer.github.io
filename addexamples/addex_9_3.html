Additional Examples 9.3

1 Carry out a Monte Carlo estimate of the probability of a random walk on the interval \([-7,6]\) escaping through the top of the interval.
The probability of escaping through the top of the interval \([-b,a]\) is \(b/(a+b) = 7/13\approx 0.5385.\) The results of a Monte Carlo simulation are shown below.
\(n\) top exits prob error
100 53 0.5300 0.0085
200 99 0.4950 0.0435
400 208 0.5200 0.0185
800 459 0.5738 0.0353
1600 872 0.5450 0.0065
3200 1748 0.5463 0.0078
6400 3473 0.5427 0.0042
