Additional Examples 1.2

1 (a) Show that \(-1, 1\) and \(2\) are fixed points of \( {\displaystyle g(x) = \frac{2+x^3-7x}{2x-6}}.\) (b) To which of \(-1, 1\) and \(2\) will Fixed Point Iteration converge? Will the convergence be faster or slower than the Bisection method?


(a) Note that \begin{eqnarray*} g(-1) &=& \frac{2+(-1)^3-7(-1)}{2(-1)-6} = -1\\ g(1) &=& \frac{2+1^3-7(1)}{2(1)-6} = 1\\ g(2) &=& \frac{2+2^3-7(2)}{2(2)-6} = 2 \end{eqnarray*} (b) The derivative is \[ g'(x) = \frac{(3x^2-7)(2x-6) - (2+x^3-7x)2}{(2x-6)^2} = \frac{4x^3-18x^2+38}{(2x-6)^2}.\] Substituting at the fixed points yields \begin{eqnarray*} g'(-1) &=& \frac{1}{4}\\ g'(1) &=& \frac{3}{2}\\ g'(2) &=& -\frac{1}{2} \end{eqnarray*} According to Theorem 1.6, Fixed Point Iteration will converge locally to the fixed points \(-1\) and \(2\).
2 Use the fpi.m code to find the three real roots of the equation \(x^5+4x^2 = \sin x + 4x^4+1\) correct to six decimal places.
One way to rearrange the equation is \[x = \frac{\sin x+4x^4+1}{x^4+4x}.\] Using this \(g(x)\) and the initial guess \(1,\) the Matlab code 
g=@(x) (sin(x)+4*x^4+1)/(x^4+4*x)
x=fpi(g,1,20)
returns the approximate root \(3.712147\) after 20 steps of Fixed Point Iteration.

With the rearrangement \[x = \frac{\sin x+1-4x^2}{x^4-4x^3}\] and initial guess \(-1,\) FPI locates the approximate root \(-0.870330\) after 80 steps. Finally, with the rearrangement \[x = \frac{-x^5+\sin x+4x^4+1}{4x}\] and initial guess \(-0.5,\) FPI locates \(-0.428275\) after 40 steps.
