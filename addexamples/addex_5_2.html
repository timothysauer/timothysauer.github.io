Additional Examples 5.2

1 Show by direct calculation that the open Newton-Cotes formula \( \int_0^b f(x) \ dx \approx \frac{b}{3} \left[ 2f(b/4)-f(b/2) + 2f(3b/4) \right] \) has degree of precision 3 .
We need to check exactness for all polynomials of degree 3 or less, and note that exactness fails for degree 4. \begin{eqnarray*} f(x) = 1: && \frac{b}{3}[2 - 1 + 2] = b = \int_0^b 1\ dx\\ f(x) = x: && \frac{b}{3}\left[2\frac{b}{4} - \frac{b}{2} + 2\frac{3b}{4}\right] = \frac{b^2}{2} = \int_0^b x\ dx\\ f(x) = x^2: && \frac{b}{3}\left[2(\frac{b}{4})^2 - (\frac{b}{2})^2 + 2(\frac{3b}{4})^2\right] = \frac{b}{3}\left[\frac{b^2}{8}-\frac{b^2}{4}+\frac{9}{8}b^2\right] =\frac{b^3}{3} = \int_0^b x^2\ dx\\ f(x) = x^3: && \frac{b}{3}\left[2(\frac{b}{4})^3 - (\frac{b}{2})^3 + 2(\frac{3b}{4})^3\right] = \frac{b}{3}\left[\frac{b^3}{32}-\frac{b^3}{8}+\frac{27}{32}b^3\right] = \frac{b^4}{4} = \int_0^b x^3\ dx\\ \end{eqnarray*}

Checking degree 4, \[f(x) = x^4: \ \frac{b}{3}\left[2(\frac{b}{4})^4 - (\frac{b}{2})^4 + 2(\frac{3b}{4})^4\right] = \frac{b}{3}\left[\frac{b^4}{128}-\frac{b^4}{16}+\frac{81}{128}b^4\right] = \frac{37b^5}{192} \neq \int_0^b x^4\ dx \]

2 Use the Composite Trapezoid Method error formula to find the number of panels required to estimate \( \int_0^1 e^{-x^2} \ dx \) to 6 correct decimal places, and carry out the estimate.


According to (5.24), the error is \( \frac{(1-0)h^2}{12}f''(c), \) where \(h=1/m\) and \( 0\lt c\lt 1.\) The second derivative is \(f''(x) = e^{-x^2}(4x^2-2),\) and so an upper bound is \(|f''(c)| < 2\) for \( 0\lt c\lt 1.\) Setting \(\frac{h^2}{12}2 < 0.5\times 10^{-6}\) yields \(h < \sqrt{3}\times 10^{-3}\) or \(m>\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\times 10^3\approx 577.4.\)

Using \(m=578\) in (5.24) results in the approximation \[\frac{1}{2m} [y_0+y_m+2\sum_{i=1}^{m-1} y_i] = \frac{1}{2m} [1+e^{-1}+2\sum_{i=1}^{m-1} e^{-(i/m)^2}] \approx 0.746824. \]
