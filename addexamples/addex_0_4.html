Additional Examples 0.4

1 Define \(f(x) = x^2-x\sqrt{x^2+9}.\) Calculate \(f(8^{12})\) correct to 3 significant digits.
When \(x=8^{12},\) the expression subtracts nearly equal numbers. An improved version of the expression is \begin{eqnarray*} f(x) &=& (x^2-x\sqrt{x^2+9}) \frac{x^2+x\sqrt{x^2+9}}{x^2+x\sqrt{x^2+9}} = \frac{x^4-x^2(x^2+9)}{x^2+x\sqrt{x^2+9}}\\ &=& -\frac{9x^2}{x^2+x\sqrt{x^2+9}} = -\frac{9}{1+\sqrt{1+9/x^2}}\approx -4.50. \end{eqnarray*}
2 Calculate both roots of \(3x^2-9^{14}x+100 = 0\) correct to 3 significant digits.
The quadratic formula gives the two roots \[x_1 = \frac{-b+\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}, \ \ \ \ \ x_2 = \frac{-b-\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\] where \(a=3, b= -9^{14},\) and \(c=100.\) The second root \(x_2\) suffers from subtraction of nearly equal numbers, and should be computed in alternative form. The roots are \begin{eqnarray*} x_1 &=& \frac{-b+\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a} \approx 7.63\times 10^{12}\\ x_2 &=& \frac{2c}{-b+\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}\approx 4.37\times 10^{-12} \end{eqnarray*}
