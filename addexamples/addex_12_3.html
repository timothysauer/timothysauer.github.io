Additional Examples 12.3

1 Find the singular values and singular vectors of the matrix \(A=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 0&1\\0&-1 \end{array}\right].\)


The eigenvalues of \( A^TA = \left[ \begin{array}{r} 0&0\\0&2 \end{array}\right] \) are \(s_1^2=2, v_1=[0,1]\) and \(s_2^2 = 0, v_2 = [1,0]\). The singular values are \(\sqrt{2}\) and \(0\).

The left singular vectors are defined by \begin{eqnarray*} \sqrt{2}u_1 &=& Av_1 = \left[ \begin{array}{r} 1\\-1 \end{array}\right]\\ u_1 &=& \left[ \begin{array}{r} 1/\sqrt{2}\\-1/\sqrt{2} \end{array}\right], \end{eqnarray*} and \(u_2 = \left[ \begin{array}{r} 1/\sqrt{2}\\1/\sqrt{2} \end{array}\right]\) is chosen to be orthogonal to \(u_1\). The SVD is \[ A=USV^T = \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 0&1\\ 0&-1\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[ \begin{array}{rr} \sqrt{2}/2&\sqrt{2}/2\\ -\sqrt{2}/2&\sqrt{2}/2 \end{array}\right] \left[ \begin{array}{rr} \sqrt{2}&0\\ 0&0\\ \end{array}\right] \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 0&1\\1&0 \end{array}\right].\]

Another perfectly good SVD for this matrix is \[ A=USV^T = \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 0&1\\ 0&-1\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[ \begin{array}{rr} -\sqrt{2}/2&\sqrt{2}/2\\ \sqrt{2}/2&\sqrt{2}/2 \end{array}\right] \left[ \begin{array}{rr} \sqrt{2}&0\\ 0&0\\ \end{array}\right] \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 0&-1\\1&0 \end{array}\right],\] illustrating the nonuniqueness comment following Theorem 12.11.

2 Find the reduced singular value decomposition of the matrix \(A = \left[ \begin{array}{rrrrrr} 6&2&10&-4&6&4\\ 8&11&5&3&8&-3 \end{array}\right] .\)

Set \(B=A^T = \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 6&8\\2&11\\10&5\\-4&3\\6&8\\4&-3 \end{array}\right]. \) The characteristic equation of the matrix \( B^TB = \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 208&144\\144&292 \end{array}\right] \) is \(\lambda^2-500\lambda + 40000 = (\lambda-400)(\lambda-100),\) showing the eigenvalues are \(s_1^2=400 \) and \(s_2^2 = 100.\) The normalized eigenvectors are \(v_1 = \left[ \begin{array}{r} 0.6\\0.8 \end{array}\right] \) and \(v_2 = \left[ \begin{array}{r} -0.8\\0.6 \end{array}\right], \) respectively. The singular values are \(20\) and \(10\).

The left singular vectors are defined by \( 20u_1 = Bv_1 = \left[ \begin{array}{r} 10\\10\\10\\0\\10\\0 \end{array}\right],\ \ \ \ \ u_1 = \left[ \begin{array}{r} 1/2\\1/2\\1/2\\0\\1/2\\0 \end{array}\right],\) and \( 10u_2 = Bv_2 = \left[ \begin{array}{r} 0\\5\\-5\\5\\0\\-5 \end{array}\right], \ \ \ \ \ u_2 = \left[ \begin{array}{r} 0\\1/2\\-1/2\\1/2\\0\\-1/2 \end{array}\right]. \) The reduced SVD of \(B\) is \[ B=USV^T = \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 1/2&0\\ 1/2&1/2\\ 1/2&-1/2\\ 0&1/2\\ 1/2&0\\ 0&-1/2 \end{array}\right] \left[ \begin{array}{cc} 20&0\\0&10 \end{array}\right] \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 0.6&0.8\\-0.8&0.6 \end{array}\right], \] and the reduced SVD of \(A\) is found by transposing the SVD of \(B\): \[ A=VSU^T = \left[ \begin{array}{rr} 0.6&-0.8\\0.8&0.6 \end{array}\right] \left[ \begin{array}{cc} 20&0\\0&10 \end{array}\right] \left[ \begin{array}{rrrrrr} 1/2&1/2&1/2&0&1/2&0\\ 0&1/2&-1/2&1/2&0&-1/2 \end{array}\right]. \]
