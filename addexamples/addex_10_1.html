Additional Examples 10.1

1 (a) Compute the Discrete Fourier Transform of the vector \(x = [1\ 2\ 3\ 2]\), and (b) apply the Inverse Discrete Fourier Transform to the result.
(a) Setting \(\omega = -i,\) the DFT is \[\frac{1}{2}\left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 1&1&1&1\\ 1&\omega&\omega^2&\omega^3\\ 1&\omega^2&\omega^4&\omega^6\\ 1&\omega^3&\omega^6&\omega^9 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\2\\3\\2 \end{array}\right] = \frac{1}{2}\left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 1&1&1&1\\ 1&-i&-1&i\\ 1&-1&1&-1\\ 1&i&-1&-i \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\2\\3\\2 \end{array}\right] = \frac{1}{2}\left[\begin{array}{r} 8\\-2\\0\\-2 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{r} 4\\-1\\0\\-1 \end{array}\right]. \]

(b) To invert the DFT, multiply by the conjugated matrix: \[\frac{1}{2}\left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 1&1&1&1\\ 1&i&-1&-i\\ 1&-1&1&-1\\ 1&-i&-1&i \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} 4\\-1\\0\\-1 \end{array}\right] = \frac{1}{2}\left[\begin{array}{r} 2\\4\\6\\4 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\2\\3\\2 \end{array}\right].\]

2 (a) Compute the Fast Fourier Transform of \(x = [5\ 10\ 5\ 0]\), and (b) apply the Inverse Fast Fourier Transform to the result.


(a) The product \(M_4 x\) is \[\frac{1}{\sqrt{4}} \left[\begin{array}{c} u+\left[\begin{array}{c} \omega^0v_0\\ \omega^1v_1 \end{array}\right] \\ u+\left[\begin{array}{c} \omega^2v_0\\ \omega^3v_1 \end{array}\right] \end{array}\right] \] where \(u=M_2\left[\begin{array}{c} x_0\\x_2 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&1\\1&-1 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{c} 5\\5 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\0 \end{array}\right] \), \(v=M_2\left[\begin{array}{c} x_1\\x_3 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&1\\1&-1 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\0 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\10 \end{array}\right], \) and \(\omega=-i.\)

Therefore \[M_4 x= \frac{1}{2} \left[\begin{array}{c} \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\0 \end{array}\right]+\left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\-10i \end{array}\right] \\ \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\0 \end{array}\right]+\left[\begin{array}{c} -10\\10i \end{array}\right] \end{array}\right] = \frac{1}{2}\left[\begin{array}{c} 20\\-10i\\0\\10i \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\-5i\\0\\5i \end{array}\right] \]

(b) To invert the transform, conjugate, apply the FFT, and conjugate the result. Thus \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\-5i\\0\\5i \end{array}\right] \rightarrow \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\5i\\0\\-5i \end{array}\right] \) and \(u=M_2\left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\0 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&1\\1&-1 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\0 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\10 \end{array}\right], v=M_2\left[\begin{array}{r} 5i\\-5i \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&1\\1&-1 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} 5i\\-5i \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{c} 0\\10i \end{array}\right]. \) Therefore \[M_4 x= \frac{1}{2} \left[\begin{array}{c} u+\left[\begin{array}{c} \omega^0v_0\\ \omega^1v_1 \end{array}\right] \\ u+\left[\begin{array}{c} \omega^2v_0\\ \omega^3v_1 \end{array}\right] \end{array}\right]= \frac{1}{2} \left[\begin{array}{c} \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\10 \end{array}\right]+\left[\begin{array}{c} 0\\10 \end{array}\right] \\ \left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\10 \end{array}\right]+\left[\begin{array}{c} 0\\-10 \end{array}\right] \end{array}\right] = \frac{1}{2}\left[\begin{array}{c} 10\\20\\10\\0 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{c} 5\\10\\5\\0 \end{array}\right] \] Conjugating the result leaves it unchanged, so the original input vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 5\\10\\5\\0 \end{array}\right] \) is recovered.
