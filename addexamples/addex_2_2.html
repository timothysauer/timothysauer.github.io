Additional Examples 2.2

1 Solve \begin{align*} \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 2&4&-2\\ 1&-2&1\\ 4&-4&8 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rrr} x_0\\x_1\\x_2 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 6\\3\\0 \end{array}\right] \end{align*} using the A=LU factorization.


Strategy: (1) Factor A into A=LU, and (2) carry out the two back-substitutions Lc=b and Ux=c. \begin{align*} \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 2&4&-2\\ 1&-2&1\\ 4&-4&8 \end{array}\right] & \longrightarrow \stackrel{\textstyle{\mbox{subtract \(\frac{1}{2}\) x row 0}}}{\mbox{from row 1}} \longrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 2&4&-2\\ 0&-4&2\\ 4&-4&8 \end{array}\right] \end{align*} \begin{align*} & \longrightarrow \stackrel{\textstyle{\mbox{subtract \(2\) x row 0}}}{\mbox{from row 2}} \longrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 2&4&-2\\ 0&-4&2\\ 0&-12&12 \end{array}\right] \end{align*} \begin{align*} & \longrightarrow \stackrel{\textstyle{\mbox{subtract \(3\) x row 1}}}{\mbox{from row 2}} \longrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 2&4&-2\\ 0&-4&2\\ 0&0&6 \end{array}\right] \end{align*} The A=LU factorization is \[ \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 2&4&-2\\ 1&-2&1\\ 4&-4&8 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1&0&0\\ \frac{1}{2}&1&0\\ 2&{3}&1 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 2&4&-2\\ 0&-4&2\\ 0&0&6 \end{array}\right]. \] Next, carry out the two back-substitutions. Solve Lc=b: \[ \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1&0&0\\ \frac{1}{2}&1&0\\ 2&{3}&1 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} c_0\\ c_1\\ c_2\\ \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r} 6\\ 3\\ 0\\ \end{array}\right] \] Starting at the top, \begin{align*} c_0&= 6\\ \frac{1}{2}(6)+c_1&= 3 \ \ \ \Rightarrow \ \ \ c_1 = 0\\ 2(6)+3(0)+c_2&= 0 \ \ \ \Rightarrow \ \ \ c_2 = -12 \end{align*} Solve Ux=c: \[ \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 2&4&-2\\ 0&-4&2\\ 0&0&6 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} x_0\\ x_1\\ x_2\\ \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{r} 6\\ 0\\ -12\\ \end{array}\right] \] Starting at the bottom, \begin{align*} 6x_2&= -12 \quad \Rightarrow\quad x_2= -2 \\ -4x_1+2(-2)&= \quad 0\quad\Rightarrow\quad x_1 = -1\\ 2x_0+4(-1)-2(-2)&= 6 \quad\Rightarrow\quad x_0 = 3 \end{align*} Therefore the solution is \(x=[3,-1,-2].\)
2 Assume that a computer can carry out an LU-factorization of a \(5000\times 5000\) matrix in 1 second. How long will it take to solve 100 problems \(Ax=b\), with the same \(3000\times 3000\) matrix \(A\) and 100 different \(b\)?
The computer can carry out \(\frac{2}{3}n^3 = \frac{2}{3}(5000)^3\) operations per second. To solve 100 problems \(Ax=b\) with the same \(A\) takes \(\frac{2}{3}(3000)^3+2(3000)^2\) for the first problem (note that two back-substitutions are required), and \(2(3000)^2\) operations for each of the remaining 99 (since we already know \(L\) and \(U\)), or a total of \(\frac{2}{3}(3000)^3+100(2)(3000)^2.\) The time required is therefore \[ \frac{\frac{2}{3}(3000)^3+200(3000)^2}{\frac{2}{3}(5000)^3} \approx 0.24\ {\rm sec.}\]
