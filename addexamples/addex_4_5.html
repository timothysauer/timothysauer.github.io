Additional Examples 4.5

1 Find the first iterate of the Gauss-Newton method applied to find the least-squares near-intersection point of the three circles of radius 1 with centers \( (-2,0), (1,1), (1, -1)\). Use initial guess \( (0,0) \).
The point \( (x,y) \) in question minimizes the sum of the squares of the residual errors \begin{eqnarray*} r_1(x,y) &=& \sqrt{(x+2)^2+y^2} - 1\\ r_2(x,y) &=& \sqrt{(x-1)^2+(y-1)^2} - 1\\ r_3(x,y) &=& \sqrt{(x-1)^2+(y+1)^2} - 1 \end{eqnarray*} The Jacobian of \( r(x,y)\) is \[ Dr(x,y) =\left[\begin{array}{rr} \frac{x+2}{\sqrt{(x+2)^2+y^2}} & \frac{y}{\sqrt{(x+2)^2+y^2}}\\ \frac{x-1}{\sqrt{(x-1)^2+(y-1)^2}} & \frac{y-1}{\sqrt{(x-1)^2+(y-1)^2}}\\ \frac{x-1}{\sqrt{(x-1)^2+(y+1)^2}} & \frac{y+1}{\sqrt{(x-1)^2+(y+1)^2}} \end{array}\right] \] The first step of the Gauss-Newton method requires solving the normal equations \( Dr(0,0)^T Dr(0,0) v^0 = -Dr(0,0)^T r(0,0) \), which are \[ \left[\begin{array}{rr} 2&0\\0&1 \end{array}\right] v_0 = -\left[\begin{array}{cc} \sqrt{2}-1\\0 \end{array}\right], \] resulting in \( x^1 = x^0 + v^0 = \left[\begin{array}{c} 0\\0 \end{array}\right]+ \left[\begin{array}{c} (1-\sqrt{2})/2\\0 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{c} (1-\sqrt{2})/2\\0 \end{array}\right] \)
2 Implement the Levenberg-Marquardt method with \(\lambda=0.1\) to fit an exponential model to the data points \( (-1, 4), (0,2), (2,1), (3, 0.5)\). Calculate the RMSE of the fit.
The goal is to minimize \( r=\left[\begin{array}{c} c_1e^{c_2t_1}-y_1\\ \vdots\\ c_1e^{c_2t_4}-y_4 \end{array}\right]. \) The Jacobian matrix with respect to the vector \( c = [c_1 \ c_2]^T \) is \( Dr = \left[\begin{array}{cc} e^{c_2t_1} & t_1c_1e^{c_2t_1}\\ \vdots\\ e^{c_2t_4} & t_4c_1e^{c_2t_4} \end{array}\right]. \)

After 15 iterations of the Levenberg-Marquardt method with initial vector \( [1,1]\), the solution \( [c_1\ c_2] = [2.271,\ -0.539] \) is reached. Substituting into the model and comparing with the data points, one calculates the RMSE to be approximately \( = 0.186. \) This RMSE is smaller than the RMSE found in Additional Example 4.2.2, where data linearization was used to approximate the nonlinear least-squares solution.
