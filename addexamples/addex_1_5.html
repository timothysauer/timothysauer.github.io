1 Apply two steps of the Secant Method on the interval with initial guesses \(x_0=1\) and \(x_1=2\) to find the approximate root of \(f(x) = 2x^3-x-7.\)
\[x_2 = x_1-\frac{f(x_1)(x_1-x_0)}{f(x_1)-f(x_0)} = 2-\frac{7(2-1)}{7-(-6)} = 2-7/13 = 19/13 \approx 1.46154\] \[ x_3 = x_2-\frac{f(x_2)(x_2-x_1)}{f(x_2)-f(x_1)} \approx 1.59108\]
2 Write a Python program that uses the Secant Method to find both roots of \(f(x) = 8x^6-12x^5+6x^4-17x^3+24x^2-12x+2.\) Is the Secant Method superlinearly convergent to both roots?
The Python program below implements the Secant Method. 
n=40
f=lambda x: 8*x**6-12*x**5+6*x**4-17*x**3+24*x**2-12*x+2;
x[0]=0
x[1]=1
for i in range(1,n):
    x[i+1] = x[i]-f(x[i])*(x[i]-x[i-1])/(f(x[i])-f(x[i-1]));
With starting guesses \(x_0=0,x_1=1\) the Secant Method converges linearly to root \(r=1/2,\) which is a multiple root. With initial guesses \(x_0=1, x_1=1.4\) the convergence is superlinear, and converges to \(r=1.2599210499.\)
