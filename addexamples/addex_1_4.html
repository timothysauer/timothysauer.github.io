Additional Examples 1.4

1 Investigate the convergence of Newton's method applied to the roots \(1\) and \(-1\) of \(f(x) = x^3-2x^2+2-1/x\). Use Theorems 1.11 and 1.12 to approximately express the error \(e_{i+1}\) in terms of \(e_i\) during convergence.


We will use the convergence theorem of Newton's method to analyze local convergence and rate of convergence. The derivatives of \(f(x)=x^3-2x^2+2-1/x\) are: \begin{eqnarray*} f'(x) &=& 3x^2-4x+x^{-2}\\ f''(x) &=& 6x-4-2x^{-3}\\ f'''(x) &=& 6+6x^{-4} \end{eqnarray*} Evaluating at the root \(r=-1.\) \begin{eqnarray*}f'(-1) &=& 3+4+1 = 8\\ f''(-1) &=& -6 -4 -2(-1) = -8 \end{eqnarray*} therefore Newton's method converges locally and quadratically to the root \(-1,\) and \(e_{i+1} \approx \left|\frac{f''(-1)}{2f'(-1)}\right| e_i^2 = \left|\frac{-8}{2(8)}\right| e_i^2 = \frac{1}{2}e_i^2.\)

The derivatives of \(f(x)\) at \(r=1\) are: \begin{eqnarray*} f'(x) &=& 3x^2-4x+x^{-2} \rightarrow f'(1) = 0\\ f''(x) &=& 6x-4-2x^{-3} \ \rightarrow f''(1) = 0\\ f'''(x) &=& 6+6x^{-4} \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \rightarrow f'''(1) = 12 \neq 0 \end{eqnarray*} Therefore \(r=1\) is a root of multiplicity 3. It follows that Newton's method converges locally and linearly to the root \(r=1\) with error \(e_{i+1} \approx \frac{m-1}{m}e_i = \frac{2}{3} e_i.\)

2 Adapt the fpi.m code from Section 1.2 to calculate all three roots of the equation \(x^5+4x^2 = \sin x + 4x^4+1\) by Newton's Method.
The Matlab code 
f=@(x) x^5+4*x^2-sin(x)-4*x^4-1;
df=@(x) 5*x^4+8*x-cos(x)-16*x^3;
g=@(x) x-f(x)/df(x);
x=fpi(g,-1,4)
applies 4 steps of Newton's Method with initial guess \(-1,\) converging to \(-0.870330\) to six decimal places. The same code with initial guess \(-0.5\) converges to \(-0.428275.\) The third root is found with initial guess \(4,\) converging to root \(3.712147\) in 4 Newton's Method steps.
