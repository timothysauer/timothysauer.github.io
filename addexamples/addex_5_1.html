Additional Examples 5.1

1 Use the three point centered-difference formula to approximate the derivative \( f'(\pi/2) \) where \( f(x) = e^{\cos x}, \) where (a) \( h = 0.1\) (b) \( h = 0.01\).

The exact derivative is \(f'(\pi/2)=e^{\cos \pi/2}(-\sin \pi/2) = -1.\)

(a) \[ \frac{f(x+h)-f(x-h)}{2h} = \frac{e^{\cos ( \pi/2+0.1)} - e^{\cos ( \pi/2-0.1)} }{2(0.1)} = -0.999993344446.\]

(b) \[ \frac{f(x+h)-f(x-h)}{2h} = \frac{e^{\cos ( \pi/2+0.01)} - e^{\cos ( \pi/2-0.01)} }{2(0.01)} = -0.999999999333.\]

2 Develop a first-order formula for estimating \( f''(x) \) that uses the data \( f(x-2h), f(x) \) and \( f(x+h) \) only.

\begin{eqnarray*} f(x-2h) &=& f(x)-2hf'(x)+\frac{4h^2}{2}f''(x)-\frac{8h^3}{6}f'''(x)+O(h^4)\\ 2f(x+h) &=& 2f(x) + 2hf'(x)+\frac{2h^2}{2}f''(x)+\frac{2h^3}{6}f'''(x)+O(h^4)\\ \frac{f(x-2h)+2f(x+h)-3f(x)}{h^2} &=& 3f''(x)-hf'''(x)+O(h^2)\\ f''(x) &=& \frac{f(x-2h)-3f(x)+2f(x+h)}{3h^2}+\frac{h}{3}f'''(x)+O(h^2) \end{eqnarray*} Therefore \(f''(x) = \frac{f(x-2h)-3f(x)+2f(x+h)}{3h^2}\) is a first-order approximation for the second derivative \(f''(x).\)
