Additional Examples 12.1

1 Let \(A=\left[\begin{array}{rr} -5&4\\-8&7 \end{array}\right].\) (a) Find all eigenvalues and eigenvectors of \(A\) using the characteristic equation. (b) Apply three steps of Power iteration with initial vector \([1,0].\) At each step, approximate the eigenvalue by the Rayleigh quotient.


The matrix \(A\) has an eigenvalue of 3 with eigenvector \([1,2]\) and an eigenvalue of \(-1\) with eigenvector \([1,1].\) At each step of the Power Method, the previous guess is multiplied by \(A\) and then normalized by dividing by its 2-norm. The Rayleigh quotient gives an estimate of the eigenvalue. The first step is \[ \left[\begin{array}{rr} -5 &4\\-8&7 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{r} -5\\-8 \end{array}\right] \rightarrow \left[\begin{array}{r} -0.5300\\-0.8480 \end{array}\right] \] with approximate eigenvalue \(-5.\)

The next two eigenvector, eigenvalue approximations are \[ \left[\begin{array}{r} -0.4008\\-0.9162 \end{array}\right],\ \ \ \ \ 1.8315 \] and \[ \left[\begin{array}{r} -0.4599\\-0.8880 \end{array}\right],\ \ \ \ \ 3.6033 \]

2 Let \(A=\left[\begin{array}{rrr} 5&2&-2\\-12&-19&12\\-12&-22&15 \end{array}\right].\) (a) Apply the Power Method with initial vector \([1,\ 2,\ 3]\) to estimate the dominant eigenvalue of \(A\). Report estimates after 10, 20, and 30 steps. (b) Apply the Inverse Power Method with shift 0 and initial vector \([1,\ 2,\ 3]\) to estimate the eigenvalue closest to zero.

(a) The powerit.m code from the text can be used to estimate the dominant eigenvalue. The command lam=powerit(A,[1;2;3],k) returns the estimates \(-7.001219110186, -7.000000254906\) and \(-7.000000000053\) for \(k=10, 20\), and 30 steps, respectively.

(b) The invpowerit.m code estimates the eigenvalue closest to zero. The command lam=invpowerit(A,[1;2;3],0,k) returns the estimates \(2.997913082747, 2.999999563017\) and \(2.999999999909\) for 10, 20, and 30 steps, respectively.
