Additional Examples 5.5

1 Translate the integral \( {\displaystyle \int_1^2 x\ln x\ dx} \) to the interval \([-1, 1]\) and approximate with degree 4 Gaussian quadrature.

\({\displaystyle \int_1^2 x\ln x\ dx = \frac{1}{2} \int_{-1}^1 f\left(\frac{t+3}{2}\right)\ dt = \frac{1}{2} \int_{-1}^1 \left(\frac{t+3}{2}\right) \ln \left(\frac{t+3}{2}\right)\ dt}.\approx \frac{1}{2}[c_1f(t_1)+c_2f(t_2)+c_3f(t_3)+c_4f(t_4)] = 0.6362943333 \), which compares to the exact integral \( 2\ln 2 - 3/4 = 0.6362943611.\)
2 Use the integral \( {\displaystyle \int_0^1 \frac{dx}{1+x^2}} \) and Gaussian quadrature of degree 4 to approximate \( \pi \). How many decimal places are correct?

Note that \( {\displaystyle \int_0^1 \frac{dx}{1+x^2}} = \pi/4\), so \(\pi\) can be approximated by multiplying the integral by \(4.\) \({\displaystyle 4\int_0^1 \frac{dx}{1+x^2} =4 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right) \int_{-1}^1 f\left(\frac{t+1}{2}\right)\ dt = 2 \int_{-1}^1 \frac{dt}{1+(\frac{t+1}{2})^2}\ }\approx 2[c_1f(t_1)+c_2f(t_2)+c_3f(t_3)+c_4f(t_4)] = 3.1416119052 \), which agrees with \( \pi \) to 4 decimal places.
