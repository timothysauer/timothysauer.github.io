Additional Examples 2.6

1 Find the Cholesky factorization of the symmetric positive-definite matrix \( \left[\begin{array}{rr} 4&-2\\-2 &6 \end{array}\right] . \)

The top row of \(R\) is \(R_{11}=\sqrt{a_{11}}=2\), followed by \( R_{12}=u=\frac{[-2]}{\sqrt{a_{11}}}=-1\).

Subtracting the outer product \(uu^T\) from the lower principal submatrix \([6]\) leaves \( 6-1=5\).

Repeating the factorization step for the remaining \(1\times 1\) matrix yields \(R_{22} = \sqrt{5}.\) Therefore \( R = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 2 &-1\\ 0&\sqrt{5} \end{array}\right] \) satisfies \[ R^TR = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 2 &0\\ -1&\sqrt{5} \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rr} 2 &-1\\ 0&\sqrt{5} \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 4 &-2\\ -2&6 \end{array}\right]. \]
