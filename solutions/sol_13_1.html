EXERCISES 13.1

\(\def\ds{\displaystyle} \def\l{\lambda}\) 1 (a) The derivative is \(f'(x)=e^x-e^{-x}\), which is zero if and only if \(x=0\). Furthermore, \(f'(x)< 0\) for \(x< 0\) and \(f'(x)> 0\) for \(x> 0\). Therefore \(f\) is unimodal on \((-\infty, \infty)\) and has an absolute minimum at \((x,y)=(0,2)\).

1 (b) The expression \(x^6\) is positive if and only if \(x\neq 0\), and equals zero at \(x=0\). Therefore \((0,0)\) is the absolute minimum. The derivative is \(f'(x)=6x^5\), which is negative for \(x< 0\) and positive for \(x> 0\), so \(f\) is unimodal on \((-\infty, \infty)\).

1 (c) The derivative \(f'(x)=8x^3+1\) is equal to zero if and only if \(x=-1/2\), is negative for \(x< -1/2\), and is positive for \(x> -1/2\). Therefore \((-1/2,-3/8)\) is the absolute minimum of the unimodal function.

1 (d) The derivative is \(f'(x)=1-1/x\), which equals zero if and only if \(x=1\), is negative for \(0< x< 1\), and positive for \(x> 1\). Thus \(f\) is unimodal on \((0,\infty)\) and the absolute minimum occurs at \((1,1)\).
