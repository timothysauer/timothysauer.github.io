\(\def\ds{\displaystyle} \) 1 (a) The Explicit Trapezoid Method is \[w_{i+1}=w_i+\frac{h}{2}[f(t_i,w_i)+f(t_i+h,w_i+hf(t_i,w_i))].\] Applied to the differential equation \(y'=f(t,y)=t\), the method is \[w_{i+1}=w_i+\frac{h}{2}[t_i+t_i+h]=w_i+h[t_i+\frac{h}{2}].\] With \(y(0)=1\) and \(h=1/4\), we calculate \begin{eqnarray*} w_0&=&y(0)=1\\ w_1&=&1+\frac{1}{4}(0)+\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^2=\frac{33}{32}\\ w_2&=&\frac{33}{32}+\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)+\frac{1}{32} = \frac{9}{8}\\ w_3&=&\frac{9}{8}+\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)+\frac{1}{32} = \frac{41}{32}\\ w_4&=&\frac{41}{32}+\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)+\frac{1}{32} = \frac{3}{2}\\ \end{eqnarray*} Global error at \(t=1\) is \(0\).

1 (b) The Explicit Trapezoid Method applied to \(y'=f(t,y)=t^2y\) is \[w_{i+1}=w_i+\frac{h}{2}[t_i^2w_i+(t_i+h)^2(w_i+h(t_i^2w_i))].\] Starting with \(y(0)=1\) we calculate \[\begin{array}{cr} t_i&w_i\ \ \ \ \\ 0&1.0000\\ 1/4&1.0078\\ 1/2&1.0477\\ 3/4&1.1587\\ 1&1.4054\\ \end{array}\] Global error at \(t=1\) is \(0.0097\).

3 (a) The second-order Taylor Method is \[w_{i+1}=w_i+hf(t_i,w_i)+\frac{h^2}{2}[f_t(t_i,w_i)+f_y(t_i,y_i)f(t_i,w_i)].\] For \(f(t,y)=ty\), the partial derivatives are \(f_t=y\) and \(f_y=t\), so the Taylor Method step is \begin{eqnarray*} w_{i+1}&=& w_i+ht_iw_i+\frac{h^2}{2}[w_i+t_i(t_iw_i)]\\ &=&w_i+ht_iw_i+\frac{h^2}{2}[w_i+t_i^2w_i] \end{eqnarray*} 3 (b) Since the partial derivatives are \(f_t=y^2\) and \(f_y=2ty+3y^2\), the second-order Taylor Method is \[w_{i+1}=w_i+h(t_iw_i^2+w_i^3)+\frac{h^2}{2}[w_i^2+(2t_iw_i+3w_i^2) (t_iw_i^2+w_i^3)].\] 3 (c) The partial derivatives are \(f_t=0\) and \(f_y=\sin y+y\cos y\). The second-order Taylor Method is \[w_{i+1}=w_i+hw_i\sin w_i+\frac{h^2}{2}(\sin w_i+w_i\cos w_i)w_i\sin w_i.\] 3 (d) The partial derivatives are \(f_t=2tye^{yt^2}\) and \(f_y=t^2e^{yt^2}\). The second-order Taylor Method is \[w_{i+1}=w_i+he^{w_it_i^2}+\frac{h^2}{2} [2t_iw_ie^{w_it_i^2}+t_i^2e^{2w_it_i^2}].\]
5 (a) At step 3, the global error satisfies \[g_3=|w_3-y_3|\leq |w_3-z_1(t_3)|+|z_1(t_3)-y_3|,\] where \(z_1(t_3)\) is the solution of the differential equation on \([t_2,t_3]\) with initial condition \(z_1(t_2)=w_2\). By definition, \(e_3=|w_3-z_1(t_3)|\) is less than the local truncation error, and \[|z_1(t_3)-y_3|\leq |z_1(t_2)-y_2|e^{Lh}=|w_2-y_2|e^{Lh}=g_2e^{Lh}.\] Therefore \[g_3\leq e_3+g_2e^{Lh}\leq e_3+(e_2+e^{Lh}e_1)e^{Lh} = e_3+e^{Lh}e_2+e^{2Lh}e_1,\] as required.

5 (b) The argument in part (a) can be continued to prove \(g_i\leq e_i+g_{i-1}e^{Lh}\). Then, using the same fact for \(g_{i-1}\) we can write \begin{eqnarray*} g_i&\leq& e_i+g_{i-1}e^{Lh}\\ &\leq& e_i+e^{Lh}(e_{i-1}+g_{i-2}e^{Lh})\\ &\leq& e_i+e^{Lh}e_{i-1}+e^{2Lh}e_{i-2}+\ldots+e^{(i-1)Lh}e_{1} \end{eqnarray*}
