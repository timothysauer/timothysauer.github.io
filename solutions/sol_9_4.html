EXERCISES 9.4

\(\def\ds{\displaystyle} \) 1 (a) We apply Ito's Formula (9.17) to \(y(t)=f(t,B_t)=tB_t+c\). Note that \(f(t,x)=tx+c\). The partial derivatives are \[\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}=x, \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}=t, \text{ and } \frac{\partial ^2 f}{\partial x^2}=0.\] Therefore Ito's Formula gives \begin{eqnarray*} dy&=&\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}(t,B_t)dt+\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(t,B_t)dB_t+\frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial ^2 f}{\partial x^2}(t,B_t)\ dB_t\ dB_t\\ &=&B_t\ dt+t\ dB_t \end{eqnarray*} as required. The initial condition is \(y(0)=0B_t+c=c\).

1 (b) The partial derivatives of \(f(t,x)=x^2-t+c\) are \[\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}=-1, \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}=2x, \text{ and } \frac{\partial ^2 f}{\partial x^2}=2.\] Ito's Formula gives \( dy=-1 dt+2B_t\ dB_t+\frac{1}{2}2\ dB_t\ dB_t =-dt+2B_t\ dB_t+dt=2B_t\ dB_t\) as required. The initial condition is \(y(0)=0^2-0+c=c.\)

3 (a) The partial derivatives of \(f(t,x)=(1+x)e^{\frac{t^2}{2}}\) are \[\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}=(1+x)te^{\frac{t^2}{2}}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}=e^{\frac{t^2}{2}}, \text{ and } \frac{\partial ^2 f}{\partial x^2}=0.\] Ito's Formula gives \[dy=(1+B_t)te^{\frac{t^2}{2}}\ dt+e^{\frac{t^2}{2}}\ dB_t=ty\ dt +e^{\frac{t^2}{2}}\ dB_t\] as required. The initial condition is \(y(0)=(1+B_0)e^{\frac{0^2}{2}}=1\).

3 (b) The partial derivatives of \(f(t,x)=x^3-3tx\) are \[\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}=-3x, \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}=3x^2-3t, \text{ and } \frac{\partial ^2 f}{\partial x^2}=6x.\] Ito's Formula gives \begin{eqnarray*} dy&=&-3B_t\ dt+(3B_t^2-3t)\ dB_t+\frac{1}{2}6B_t\ dB_t\ dB_t\\ &=& (-3B_t+3B_t)\ dt+(3B_t^2-3t)\ dB_t\\ &=&(3B_t^2-3t)\ dB_t \end{eqnarray*} as required. The initial condition is \(y(0)=B_0^3-3(0)B_0=0.\)

5 The partial derivatives of \(f(t,x)=\ln(2x+e^{y_0})\) are \[\frac{\partial f}{\partial t}=0, \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}=\frac{2}{2x+e^{y_0}}, \text{ and } \frac{\partial ^2 f}{\partial x^2}=\frac{-4}{(2x+e^{y_0})^2}.\] Noting that \(e^y=2x+e^{y_0}\), Ito's Formula gives \begin{eqnarray*} dy&=&\frac{2}{e^y}\ dB_t+\frac{1}{2}\frac{-4}{e^{2y}}\ dB_t\ dB_t\\ &=& 2e^{-y}\ dB_t-2e^{-2y}\ dt \end{eqnarray*} as required.
