\(\def\ds{\displaystyle} \) 1 (a) Let \(f(x)=x^2, a=0, b=1\). Following the definition of Romberg Integration, \[R_{11} = (b-a)\frac{f(a)+f(b)}{2} = \frac{0^2+1^2}{2}=\frac{1}{2}.\] The remainder of the first column is computed from the Composite Trapezoid Rule with \(h_2=(b-a)/2=1/2\), \[R_{21}=\frac{1}{2}R_{11}+h_2f(a+h_2)=\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{2}+ \frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{4}=\frac{3}{8}\] and \(h_3=(b-a)/4=1/4\), \[ R_{31}=\frac{1}{2}R_{21}+h_3[f(a+h_3)+f(a+3h_3)]=\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{3}{8} +\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)^2+\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)^2 =\frac{11}{32}.\] The second column consists of extrapolations of the first column \begin{eqnarray*} R_{22} &=& \frac{4R_{21}-R_{11}}{4-1}=\frac{4(\frac{3}{8})-\frac{1}{2}}{3} =\frac{1}{3}\\ R_{32} &=& \frac{4R_{31}-R_{21}}{4-1}=\frac{4(\frac{11}{32})-\frac{3}{8}}{3} =\frac{1}{3}, \end{eqnarray*} and the third column extrapolates the second column \begin{eqnarray*} R_{33} &=& \frac{4^2R_{32}-R_{22}}{4^2-1}=\frac{16(\frac{1}{3})-\frac{1}{3}}{15} =\frac{1}{3} \end{eqnarray*} The Romberg triangle is \[\begin{array}{ccc} R_{11}=\frac{1}{2}\\ \\ R_{21}=\frac{3}{8}&R_{22}=\frac{1}{3}\\ \\ R_{31}=\frac{11}{32}&R_{32}=\frac{1}{3}&R_{33}=\frac{1}{3} \end{array}\] 1 (b) Let \(f(x)=\cos x, a=0, b=\pi/2\). 3 Notice that \(R_{11}=(b-a)(f(a)+f(b))/2 = h_2(f(a)+f(b))\). Then \begin{eqnarray*} R_{22}&=&\frac{4R_{21}-R_{11}}{4-1}\\ &=&\frac{4(\frac{1}{2}R_{11}+h_2f(a+h_2))-R_{11}}{3}\\ &=&\frac{R_{11}+4h_2f(a+h_2)}{3}\\ &=&\frac{h_2(f(a)+f(b))+4h_2f(a+h_2)}{3}\\ &=&\frac{h_2}{3}[f(a)+4f(a+h_2)+f(b)] \end{eqnarray*} is Simpson's Rule on \([a,b]\).
