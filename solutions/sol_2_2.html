\(\def\ds{\displaystyle} \) 1 (a) Subtracting \(3\) times the top row from the second row yields the upper triangular matrix \(\ds U= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&2\\ 0&-2\\ \end{array}\right].\) The matrix of multipliers is \(\ds L=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\ 3&1\\ \end{array}\right].\) Check by multiplication: \[LU = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\ 3&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&2\\ 0&-2\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&2\\ 3&4\\ \end{array}\right]. \] 1 (b) Subtracting \(2\) times the top row from the second row yields \[ U=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&3\\ 0&-4\\ \end{array}\right] \text{ and } L=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\ 2&1\\ \end{array}\right].\] Check by multiplication: \[LU = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\ 2&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&3\\ 0&-4\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&3\\ 2&2\\ \end{array}\right]. \] 1 (c) Subtracting \(\ds -\frac{5}{3}\) times the top row from the second row yields \[ U=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 3&-4\\ 0&-\frac{14}{3}\\ \end{array}\right] \text{ and } L=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\ -\frac{5}{3}&1\\ \end{array}\right].\] Check by multiplication: \[LU = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\ -\frac{5}{3}&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rr} 3&-4\\ 0&-\frac{14}{3}\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 3&-4\\ -5&2\\ \end{array}\right]. \]
3 (a) Subtracting \(2\) times the top row from the second row gives the factorization \[ LU = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\ 2&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rr} 3&7\\ 0&-13\\ \end{array}\right]. \] Solving \(Lc=b\), or \[ \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\ 2&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} c_0\\ c_1\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1\\ -11\\ \end{array}\right], \] yields \(c_0=1\) and \(2(1)+c_1=-11\), or \(c_1=-13\). Solving \(Ux=c\), or \[ \left[\begin{array}{rr} 3&7\\ 0&-13\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} x_0\\ x_1\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1\\ -13\\ \end{array}\right], \] gives \(x_1=1\) and \(3x_0+7=1\), or \(x_0=-2\). Thus \(x=[-2,1]\).

3 (b) Subtracting \(2\) times the top row from the second row gives the factorization \[ LU = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\ 2&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rr} 2&3\\ 0&1\\ \end{array}\right]. \] Solving \(Lc=b\), or \[ \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\ 2&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} c_0\\ c_1\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1\\ 3\\ \end{array}\right], \] yields \(c_0=1\) and \(2(1)+c_1=3\), or \(c_1=1\). Solving \(Ux=c\), or \[ \left[\begin{array}{rr} 2&3\\ 0&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} x_0\\ x_1\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1\\ 1\\ \end{array}\right], \] gives \(x_1=1\) and \(2x_0+3(1)=1\), or \(x_0=-1\). Thus \(x=[-1,1]\).

5 Since \(A\) is already factored as \(LU\), only the back substitution is needed. Solving \(Lc=b\), or \[\left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 1&0&0&0\\ 0&1&0&0\\ 1&3&1&0\\ 4&1&2&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} c_0\\c_1\\c_2\\c_3\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\1\\2\\0\\ \end{array}\right], \] yields \(c_0=1, c_1=1, c_2= -2\), and \(c_3= -1\). Solving \(Ux=c\), or \[\left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 2&1&0&0\\ 0&1&2&0\\ 0&0&-1&1\\ 0&0&0&1\\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} x_0\\x_1\\x_2\\x_3\\ \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\1\\-2\\-1\\ \end{array}\right], \] yields \(x_3= -1, x_2=1, x_1= -1\), and \(x_0= 1\). Thus \(x=[1, -1,1, -1]\).
7 To solve 10000 upper-triangular \(500\times 500\) systems requires 10000 back substitutions, or \(10000(500)^2\) operations. To solve one full \(5000\times 5000\) system requires approximately \(2(5000)^3/3+2(5000)^2\approx 2(5000)^3/3\) operations. The number of seconds to solve the latter is the ratio \[ \frac{2(5000)^3/3}{10000(500)^2} = \frac{100}{3}\approx 33.33 \text{ seconds}, \] (or 33.38 seconds if the \(2(5000)^2\) term is not neglected).
9 The first problem \(Ax=b_0\) requires approximately \(2n^3/3\) multiplications, while the 100 subsequent problems require \(2n^2\) each. Setting the two equal gives the equation \({\ds \frac{2n^3}{3}=200n^2,}\) or \(n=300\).
