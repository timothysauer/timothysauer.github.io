\(\def\ds{\displaystyle} \) 1 (a) The residual is \({\ds r = b-Ax_0 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\0 \end{array}\right] = q_1=r}\).

\({\ds y=Aq_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\1 \end{array}\right]}\) and we orthogonalize by \({\ds y'=y-q_1q_1^Ty = y-h_{11}q_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\1 \end{array}\right], h_{11}=1}\), and so \({\ds q_2= \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\1 \end{array}\right]}\) and \(h_{21}=1\). The first estimate \(x_1\) is found by finding the number \(c_1\) that best fits \({\ds \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\1 \end{array}\right] c_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0 \end{array}\right]}\), which from the normal equations is \(c_1=1/2\). Therefore \({\ds x_1 = Q_1c_1+x_0= \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\0 \end{array}\right] (1/2)+ \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{c} 1/2\\0\\0 \end{array}\right] }\).

In step two, we find \({\ds y=Aq_2 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\1 \end{array}\right]}\) and orthogonalize by \begin{eqnarray*} y'&=&y-q_1q_1^Ty= y-h_{12}q_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\1 \end{array}\right], h_{12}=0\\ y''&=&y'-q_2q_2^Ty= y-h_{22}q_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right], h_{22}=1. \end{eqnarray*} Therefore \(h_{32}=0\), terminating the iteration. The second and final estimate \(x_2\) is found by finding the 2-vector \(c_2\) that best fits \({\ds \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\1&1\\0&0 \end{array}\right] c_2 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]}\), which from the normal equations is \(c_2=[1, -1]^T\). Finally \({\ds x_2 = Q_2c_2+x_0= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\0&0\\0&1 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\-1 \end{array}\right]+ \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\-1 \end{array}\right] }\).

1 (b) The residual is \({\ds r = b-Ax_0 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\0 \end{array}\right] = q_1=r}\).

\({\ds y=Aq_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\-1\\0 \end{array}\right]}\) and we orthogonalize by \({\ds y'=y-q_1q_1^Ty = y-h_{11}q_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\-1\\0 \end{array}\right], h_{11}=1}\), and so \({\ds q_2= \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\-1\\0 \end{array}\right]}\) and \(h_{21}=1\). The first estimate \(x_1\) is found by finding the number \(c_1\) that best fits \({\ds \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\1 \end{array}\right] c_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0 \end{array}\right]}\), which from the normal equations is \(c_1=1/2\). Therefore \({\ds x_1 = Q_1c_1+x_0= \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\0 \end{array}\right] (1/2)+ \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{c} 1/2\\0\\0 \end{array}\right] }\).

In step two, we find \({\ds y=Aq_2 = \left[\begin{array}{r} -1\\-1\\0 \end{array}\right]}\) and orthogonalize by \begin{eqnarray*} y'&=&y-q_1q_1^Ty= y-h_{12}q_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\-1\\0 \end{array}\right], h_{12}=-1\\ y''&=&y'-q_2q_2^Ty= y-h_{22}q_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right], h_{22}=1. \end{eqnarray*} Therefore \(h_{32}=0\), terminating the iteration. The second and final estimate \(x_2\) is found by finding the 2-vector \(c_2\) that best fits \({\ds \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&-1\\1&1\\0&0 \end{array}\right] c_2 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]}\), which from the normal equations is \(c_2=[1/2, -1/2]^T\). Finally \({\ds x_2 = Q_2c_2+x_0= \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1&0\\0&-1\\0&0 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} 1/2\\-1/2 \end{array}\right]+ \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{r} 1/2\\1/2\\0 \end{array}\right] }\).

1 (c) The residual is \({\ds r = b-Ax_0 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\0 \end{array}\right] = q_1=r}\).

\({\ds y=Aq_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\1\\0 \end{array}\right]}\) and we orthogonalize by \({\ds y'=y-q_1q_1^Ty = y-h_{11}q_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\1\\0 \end{array}\right], h_{11}=0}\), and so \({\ds q_2= \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\1\\0 \end{array}\right]}\) and \(h_{21}=1\). The first estimate \(x_1\) is found by finding the number \(c_1\) that best fits \({\ds \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\1 \end{array}\right] c_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0 \end{array}\right]}\), which from the normal equations is \(c_1=0\). Therefore \({\ds x_1 = Q_1c_1+x_0= \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]}\).

In step two, we find \({\ds y=Aq_2 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\1 \end{array}\right]}\) and orthogonalize by \begin{eqnarray*} y'&=&y-q_1q_1^Ty= y-h_{12}q_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\1 \end{array}\right], h_{12}=0\\ y''&=&y'-q_2q_2^Ty= y-h_{22}q_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\1 \end{array}\right], h_{22}=0. \end{eqnarray*} Therefore \(h_{32}=1\). The second estimate \(x_2\) is found by finding the vector \(c_2\) that best fits \({\ds \left[\begin{array}{rr} 0&0\\1&0\\0&1 \end{array}\right] c_2 = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]}\), which from the normal equations is \({\ds c_2= \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0 \end{array}\right] }\).

Therefore \({\ds x_2 = Q_2c_2+x_0= \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]}\).

In step three, we find \({\ds y=Aq_3 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]}\) and orthogonalize by \begin{eqnarray*} y'&=&y-q_1q_1^Ty= y-h_{12}q_1 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right], h_{13}=1 \end{eqnarray*} and \(h_{23}=h_{33}=h_{43}=0\). The third and final estimate \(x_3\) is found by finding the 3-vector \(c_3\) that best fits \({\ds \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 0&0&1\\1&0&0\\0&1&0\\0&0&0 \end{array}\right] c_3 = \left[\begin{array}{r} 1\\0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]}\), which from the normal equations is \({\ds c_3= \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\1 \end{array}\right]. }\)

Finally \({\ds x_3 = Q_3c_3+x_0= \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1&0&0\\0&1&0\\0&0&1 \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\1 \end{array}\right]+ \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\0 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{r} 0\\0\\1 \end{array}\right] }\).

3 GMRES terminates at the correct solution after two steps because \(h_{32}=0\) in the \(k=2\) step, since the Modified Gram Schmidt orthogonalization in the inner loop results in \(y=0\). To show this, it suffices to prove that \(A^2r\) belongs to span \(\langle r, Ar \rangle\). Write the matrix \[A= \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 1&0&a_{13}\\ 0&1&a_{23}\\ 0&0&1 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{cc} I&a\\ 0&I \end{array}\right] \] in block form where \({\ds a= \left[\begin{array}{r} a_{13}\\a_{23} \end{array}\right], }\) and the residual as \({\ds r= \left[\begin{array}{ccc} r_1\\ r_2 \end{array}\right] }\) where \(r_1\) is a 2-vector and \(r_2\) is a scalar. In block form we can multiply \[Ar = \left[\begin{array}{cc} I&a\\0&I \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{c} r_1\\r_2 \end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{c} r_1+ar_2\\r_2 \end{array}\right]= r+ \left[\begin{array}{c} ar_2\\0 \end{array}\right],\] and \[ A^2r=Ar+A \left[\begin{array}{c} ar_2\\0 \end{array}\right]= r+\left[\begin{array}{c} ar_2\\0 \end{array}\right]+\left[\begin{array}{c} ar_2\\0 \end{array}\right]= r+2\left[\begin{array}{c} ar_2\\0 \end{array}\right], \] showing that \(A^2r\) belongs to span \(\langle r, Ar \rangle\).
